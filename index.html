• 1200 A.D.

    Our Ancestry

    A group of Vikings moved to the Northeast Corner of England. Their names at the time are not known.

  • 1400 A.D.

    How The Wood Name Was Born

    200 years later, this group of Vikings were known as the Bull family and were Royal Executioners. Part of the Bull family did not like being associated with Excutioners and left changing their last name to Wood. This is when the Wood family name is born.

  • 1700's

    How The Wood Family Ended Up In America

    The 1700's marks the first time a Wood family member traveled to America, an Englishman fleeing with a Spanish Women in seek of religious freedom and to get married.

  • 1700-1800's

    Living In The U.S.

    The Wood family had lived althroughout the United States as the years went by. Living in different places like Pennsylvania, Arkansas, and Missouri

  • 1889

    How The Wood Family Reached Washington State

    George Wood a son living on his fathers farm, decided he wanted to make a life for himself and left. Initially trying to get land in Oklahoma during the land rush but was unsuccesful. George continued onwards dispite losing out on a huge oppurtunity and landed in Washington State.

  • 1890's

    Settling In

    George had found himself a prime piece of land near a place called Nesika. Which later would be flooded and currently resides under Riffe Lake along with two other towns.

  • 1900's

    The Beginning and the End

    George would go on to have a couple kids dying in the 1900's, his kids however, would then go on to have their own kids and the cycle began. George is my great great grandfather, I never got to meet him but I thank him for bringing our family to Washington where I would grow up with the rest of the Wood family.
